RAG that cites its sources, or it doesn’t ship

A retrieval answer without a citation is a confident guess. If a user can’t trace a claim back to the chunk it came from, you can’t either — and neither can the eval.

Carry the source through

Every chunk keeps its identity from ingestion to answer: a stable chunk_id, its document, and a span. The model is asked to attach those ids to each claim, and the UI turns them into links.

{
  "claim": "The retry budget defaults to 3.",
  "source": "docs/agents.md#L82",
  "score": 0.91
}

Refuse when there’s nothing to cite

  • Below a retrieval-score floor, say “I don’t have a source for that.”
  • Never let the model paper over a gap with fluent prose.
  • Log the misses — they’re your content backlog.
Auditable beats plausible. A cited “I’m not sure” is worth more than an uncited certainty.

Citations aren’t a nice-to-have bolted on at the end. They’re the difference between a demo and something a team will actually trust.
